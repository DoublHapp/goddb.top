# Designing content systems for the long run

The most overlooked part of a personal site is not its visual design, but how its content continues to evolve. A reliable content system keeps clear boundaries between data structures, routes, and presentation components.

## Start with stable identities

Every article and project uses a unique slug. Titles may change and languages may expand, but public URLs should remain stable whenever possible.

## Keep content close to code

Build-time content has explicit version history and follows the same review process as code. For a small site without editorial workflows, this is a simple and dependable choice.

## Bilingual does not mean duplicated pages

Language is part of the content context. Navigation, SEO metadata, and article bodies should share one source of truth to avoid mixed-language screens.

The goal is not a complex system. It is making every content addition predictable.
